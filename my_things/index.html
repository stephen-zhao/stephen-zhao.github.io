about

Who I Am

I'm Stephen! I'm a sophomore studying Computer Science at the University of Waterloo in the David Cheriton School of Computer Science. I'm quite an introverted person; however, I often push myself outside of my comfort zone to seek more exciting opportunities. I appreciate fact-checked, semantically strong, arguments over unjustified garbage. I don't like meat. I almost always prefer covers to originals. I use the tab-key, but only as a shortcut to generate space characters. I watch a bunch of educational youtube (in particular, CGP Grey and Historia Civilis). And I love r/ImaginaryCastles.

What I Do

In my free time, I really enjoy experimenting with sandbox games. Creating large, well-organized systems have always interested me. Building aesthetically pleasing scenes and complex transportation networks in SimCity 4 and Cities:Skylines is a go-to pastime. I also do a little bit of digital photography, illustration manipulation, PC building, music making, and game modding. I hope to eventually save up for flight school to fulfill my long-time dream to soar the skies.

What I Strive For

I also spend a lot of time thinking about what the future holds. I believe in the will of our species to take action and develop strategies to cope with the globally-present struggles we face today. These include the iminent danger that is the consequence of climate change, and the ongoing detriment to the advancement of our species as a whole that is the consequence of unequal opportunity and lack of education in some parts of the world. As a Canadian and a citizen of the Earth, to preserve our history and to build us into a spacefaring civilization, I have an extended vision to contribute something genuinely useful to humanity.

The Stephen Zhao Logo

My logo—which appears on the home page(and sidebar if your browser is wide enough)—represents, in a broad sense, who I am and what I stand for. Although it may seem a bit cryptic, with a bit of staring you would notice that the logo is actually comprised of two letters: a blue 'S' and a red 'Z', my primary initials. The 'S' is textured as our blue marble, the Earth. This is meant to indicate the responsibility I feel to bettering our planet. The 'Z' is textured as a red PCB. This shows my love for technology and computer science.

The two surround a white core, which represents my fundamental beliefs and the causes I rally behind. I believe that stronger science and technological advancement is the key to bettering quality of life across the world, and that it is a global effort to reach for this continued goal. I believe we must stop human-induced climate change and make emerging technologies globally accessible.In particular, I'm looking forward to the proliferation of self-driving vehicles, the promises of cheaper renewable energy sources (solar is cheaper than wind now, and the trend is still evident), and the endeavors to make internet access a universal right.
