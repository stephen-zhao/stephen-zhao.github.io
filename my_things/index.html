Who I am

I'm Stephen! I'm a sophomore studying Computer Science at the University of Waterloo in the David Cheriton School of Computer Science. I'm quite an introverted person; however, I often push myself outside of my comfort zone to seek more exciting opportunities. In my free time, I really enjoy experimenting with sandbox games. Creating large, well-organized systems have always interested me. Building aesthetically pleasing scenes and complex transportation networks in SimCity 4 and Cities:Skylines is a go-to pastime. I also do a little bit of digital photography, illustration manipulation, PC building, music making, and game modding. I hope to eventually save up for flight school to fulfill my long-time dream to soar the skies. I hold firm beliefs that we must do everything we can to combat human-induced climate change and to provide fair opportunity to education and global access to technology.

The Stephen Zhao Logo

I created my logo—which appears on the home page(and sidebar if your browser is wide enough)—in my junior year of secondary school. I had in mind of a simple design which would make an attempt  to present my character. The circular figure actually comprises of a blue S and a red Z, my primary initials. The S is textured as our blue marble, the Earth. This is meant to indicate the responsibility I feel to bettering our planet. The Z is textured as a red PCB. This shows my love for technology and computer science.

The two surround a white core, which represents my fundamental beliefs and the causes I rally behind. I believe that stronger science and technological advancement is the key to bettering quality of life across the world, and that it is a global effort to reach for this continued goal. I believe we must stop human-induced climate change and make emerging technologies globally accessible. In particular, I'm looking forward to the proliferation of self-driving vehicles, the promises of cheaper renewable energy sources (solar is cheaper than wind now, and the trend is still evident), and the endeavors to make internet access a universal right.
