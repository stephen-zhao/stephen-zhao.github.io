about

Who I Am

I'm Stephen! I'm a sophomore studying Computer Science at the University of Waterloo. I think—and those around me can vouch for it—that I'm quite introverted; however, I often push myself outside of my comfort zone to seek more exciting opportunities. I haven't yet fully probed at the subject of Computer Science and its co-subjects and nearby derivatives; therefore, my career path is still a fork in the road and my knowledgebase is yet to be specialized. I harness a variety of skills earned and demonstrated in my previous work experiences and side projects, both of which are listed below.

What I Do

In my free time, I really enjoy experimenting in construction, artistic, sandbox games such as SimCity, where I can plan out magnificent metropolitan regions and sprawling landscapes; and Minecraft, where I can design architectural wonders and intricate redstone circuitry. Creating large, well-organized systems always interested me. I also do a little bit of digital photography, PC building, music making, and game modding. I hope to eventually save up for flight school to fulfill my long-time dream to soar the skies.

What I Strive For

I also spend a lot of time thinking about what the future holds. I believe in the will of our species to take action and develop strategies to cope with the globally-present struggles we face today. These include the iminent danger that is the consequence of human-induced climate change, and the ongoing detriment to the advancement of our species as a whole that is the consequence of unequal opportunity, terrorism and warfare, and lack of education in some parts of the world. As a Canadian and a citizen of the Earth, to preserve our history and to build us into a spacefaring civilization, I've extended my goal beyond the day-to-day "make enough to get by" routine to contributing something genuinely useful to humanity.

The Stephen Zhao Logo

My logo—which appears on the home page and sidebar (if your browser is wide enough)—represents, in a broad sense, who I am and what I stand for. Although it may seem a bit cryptic, with a bit of staring you would notice that the logo actually comprises of two letters: a blue 'S' and a red 'Z'. These are my primary initials.

The 'S' is textured as our blue marble, the Earth. This indicates the responsibility I feel towards bettering our planet; I want to eradicate world hunger; stop human-induced climate change; and, most of all, accelerate advanced science and technology research while making emerging technologies accessible globally. Worldwide "anytime-anywhere" access to free Internet is an example of what I wish for the world to achieve.

This leads us into the 'Z', which is textured as a red PCB. This shows my love for technology and computer science. It represents what I am studying right now along with where many of my interests lie.
